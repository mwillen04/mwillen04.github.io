The Need For Change in the United States Criminal Justice System

by: Mirical Brooks

Although the U.S. poulation only has 37% of people of color, 67% of them are behind bars. (It's Time to Reform the Criminal (In)Justice System, Ben & Jerry's).

How Did We Get Here?

Dating back to the birth of America, we see the founding fathers creating what we call the "Bill of Rights". According to the eigth amendment, "cruel & unusual" punishment was not allowed, therefore the idea of incarceration took place. People who would be placed in jail would be given a Bible and basic neccessities while living in a confined space rather than being whipped in public or getting their ears cut off. (Foundations of Criminal Justice) While this seems like a good idea, the people of color in America were not treated in this manner. Starting in the 1800s, some of the earliest police were "slave patrols".

"I [patroller's name], do swear, that I will as searcher for guns, swords, and other weapons among the slaves in my district, faithfully, and as privately as I can, discharge the trust reposed in me as the law directs, to the best of my power. So help me God." - Slave Patroller's Oath, North Carolina, 1828. (Slave Patrols: An Early Form of Policing, Hansen)

In short, a slave patroller had to do three things...

  • chase down slaves, apprehend them, then return them to their owners
  • provide a form of terror to deter slave revolts
  • maintain a form of dicipline for slaves who were subject to summary justice (outside of law)

    • (Slave Patrols: An Early Form of Policing, Hansen)

    This is nothing like the punishment given to those incarcerated as it did not give a slave breathing room to "rest" or "read a Bible". They were tormented pyschological and physically to work for people who hated them and only tolerated them because they were money-makers. A well written essay by a student of the University of Arkansas divulged, "Slavery was used as a method to undermine blacks to maintain a level of superiority and security. The prison system is a bigger scale of the significant racialized system. Although slavery has been deemed unconstitutional, our current penal system has some disturbingly similar characteristics." (Racial Prejudice in the Criminal Justice System, Cooper). America is built off of making others work for them, just like their motherland. Despite being the "land of the free", it is evident people of color were not included in the song "The Star Spangled Banner" when it was first made.

    Even after slavery, people of color, specifically African-Americans have continued to face police brutality for decades and unfair treatment in the justice system. One example of this is the "Scottboro Boys".

    In the year of 1932, two white woman accussed nine black boys, ranging from ages 12-19 of raping them on a train (All White Juries). These fasely accussed boys were retried on trial many times by all-white juries and ultimately eight of theboys were sentneced to death for a crime they did not commit. The sad truth is this was the fate of many African American boys in these times. If they spoke the "wrong way", looked the "wrong way", or breathed the "wrong way", they would be convicted of something and killed. In a way, it is like that today. African Americans are consistantly racially profiled and are forced to assimilate themselves in order to not fit a negative stereotype.

    Are We Still Being Affected Today?

    A young man strolled down an inactive street on August 9th of 2014. People waved and smiled when he passed their home. His mind rested, starting to ease knowing his mother would have some medicine to relieve her migraine. That same day on the news, an unarmed teen named Mike Brown hurled over the ground after a police man shot him. The young man, who brought his mother medicine, went by the name of Mike Brown. Before he closed his eyes for the last time, he cried out, pleading for his life from more than six feet away from the officer with his hands in the air. Prior to his death, police men sought to find a tall, black man, that was wide in size. Unluckily, Mike Brown fitted this same description and due to his “thug appearance”, says Officer Wilson, death knocked on his door.

    In more recent accounts the names of George Floyd, Rayshard Brooks, Daniel Prude, Breonna Taylor, Atitiana Jefferson, Stephon Clark, Botham Jean, Patrick Warren, Vincent Belmote, Angelo Quinto, Anjelo Crooms, Sincere Pierce, Jonathan Price, Dreasjon Reed, William Greene, Manuel Elijah Ellis, Elijah McClain, etc...I could continue ages on the names of African Americans who have faced police brutality within this year and the previous years before that, but when will the list stop?

    The efforts people of all ethnicities that have created to stand up for what is right does not go unnoticed. The sweat, blood, and tears they have let drip down their body while advocating for equal rights will always be remebered. Their marches of faith, of hope, and courage is well respected. However, truly, no change to the U.S. judicial system has been made yet.

    Why Do We "Need" A Change?

    When recalling the issue at hand, people of color are not advocating to be free of any type of punishment when conducting crimes, and I believe that is where the confusion stems from. Rather, they want to be treated equally for the crimes they have committed. There is no reason why a twelve year old boy was shot dead in the forehead within two seconds because he was playing with a toy gun, but Tamir Rice was. In contrast, it is okay for a seventeen-year-old to shoot protestors with a rifle and still live, his name is Kyle Rittenhouse. Take a guess at which one was not a person of color. Just by guessing that Tamir Rice must be the person of color just with the mere coverage of the story, you have proved the criminal justice system is racially targeted.

    When Can You Help?

    Now. To help fight racism and discrimination in the criminal justice system, the use of educating people through social media is vital. Due to many people being trapped in their homes during the pandemic, it gives people time to think, reflect, and take action. The more people that are notified about the things going on, the more connections that will be made towards helping this issue. America has great potential, however if it still repeats its past it will never be able to get to a brighter future. It’s time to utilize the technology that is made to better the society.
